Best current route

Most inquiries are best handled through the review and follow-up request flow, because that route keeps the relevant business context attached to the request.

Why this route works

It keeps the signal intact

The in-product handoff carries the session summary, findings, and selected package intent with it. That makes it easier to review fit, scope, and the actual operating issue instead of starting from scratch.

What to do

Complete the review, then open follow-up

  1. Run the diagnostic review on the homepage.
  2. Read the findings and choose the next-step option that is closest to what you want.
  3. Open the contact request and explain what you want help with first.

Which path fits your question?

Because the best response depends on context, the site currently routes most inquiries through the same follow-up path. What changes is the type of note you send and the context you include.

General inquiries

Use the review to establish fit

If you want to understand what Operational Clarity does, whether the approach is relevant, or where to start, the diagnostic review is the best entry point. It creates enough context for a more useful response.

Support or privacy questions

Reference the related submission

If your question relates to information you already submitted, the review flow, or a privacy concern, reference that submission when possible. If you do not already have an active thread, use the in-product follow-up request and label the note clearly as support or privacy.

Business inquiries

Request next-step help after findings

If you want a diagnostic review, an operations sprint, ongoing advisory support, or a commercial discussion, complete the review first and open the follow-up request after your findings appear.

Helpful details to include

A short, specific note is usually enough to route the request well.

  • Whether your note is a general question, a support or privacy issue, or a business inquiry.
  • Your name, company, and the best email to use for follow-up if you want a response.
  • A short description of the operating issue, question, or concern you want addressed.
  • If the note relates to an earlier submission, include the approximate submission date and any identifying context you already used.

What happens next

Requests are reviewed by the team operating Operational Clarity and routed based on the type of inquiry.

  • Business inquiries are reviewed for fit, scope, and the most useful next step.
  • Support and privacy questions are reviewed against the relevant session when enough identifying detail is provided.
  • If a request is a fit for follow-up, the next step is confirmed directly from there.
  • Response timing depends on workload and the nature of the request, so this page does not promise fixed office hours or instant turnaround.
