Privacy Policy

Operational Clarity lets users work through an AI-assisted business diagnostic, stay anonymous by default, and choose whether to request follow-up. This page explains, in plain English, what information may be collected, how it may be used, and how the service handles that information today.

What this policy covers

This policy applies to the public Operational Clarity site, the diagnostic review flow, and the follow-up request path used when someone asks EHI Studio for next-step help.

  • Operational Clarity is a software-led business diagnostic experience developed by EHI Studio.
  • The service is designed so a user can stay anonymous unless they choose to submit contact details for follow-up.
  • The Last updated line at the top of this page shows when this policy was last materially revised.

Information we may collect

We collect the information needed to run the review, present results, support follow-up, and understand how the service is being used.

Information you provide

Inputs and follow-up details

  • Messages entered into the review, including transcript-style business inputs and follow-up responses.
  • If you request next-step help, details such as name, email, company, phone if provided, message text, and package-selection interest.
  • Any additional context you choose to share in a support, privacy, or business inquiry.

Session context

Operational and usage details

  • Session identifiers, timestamps, stage progression, and page URL.
  • Source or channel details when they are available.
  • Whether contact details were submitted and which package or next-step option appears to be of interest.

Generated from the session

Outputs and derived fields

  • Assistant replies and the diagnostic outputs shown back to the user.
  • Extracted fields, findings, normalized tags, recommendations, confidence fields, and product-learning or analytics fields tied to the session.
  • Anonymous benchmarking and archive snapshots used to restore sessions and compare patterns over time.

Stored in the browser

Local session persistence

  • The active review session is stored in your browser so progress can be restored after a reload.
  • Anonymous history and archive data may also be stored locally for restore behavior and benchmarking.
  • If an external lead-capture route is unavailable, submitted contact requests may remain stored locally in your browser.

How information may be used

Information submitted through Operational Clarity may be used to operate the service, support follow-up, and improve the experience over time.

  • To run the diagnostic, generate outputs, and maintain the current session.
  • To restore sessions, compare anonymous patterns, and understand how the product is being used.
  • To review quality, troubleshoot issues, improve prompts, improve the product, and support internal analytics.
  • To assess package-selection intent and prepare for a follow-up conversation when a user requests help.
  • To route contact requests through internal review or notification workflows used to operate the service.
  • We do not sell personal information.

AI processing and internal review

Operational Clarity relies on third-party infrastructure to generate responses and operate the service.

AI generation

Review inputs help generate outputs

The review conversation is sent to the OpenAI service used to generate diagnostic responses. Any information entered into the review may be processed for that purpose.

Storage and analytics

Session records may be synced

Session data is stored in your browser and may also be synced to Supabase for product operations, analytics, and internal review.

Human review

Follow-up can involve EHI Studio

If you request next-step help, relevant submissions and findings may be reviewed by people at EHI Studio for intake, support, advisory follow-up, or service improvement.

Website analytics

Limited public-site tracking

The public site uses browser-based session storage and does not include third-party advertising pixels or public web analytics scripts.

Please avoid highly sensitive information

Operational Clarity is designed for business diagnostic context, not for collecting highly sensitive records.

Please do not submit passwords, bank or payment details, government IDs, medical information, legal claims, employee personal data, or other highly sensitive records unless EHI Studio specifically asks for them for a defined follow-up engagement.

Security, retention, and updates

Security

Reasonable safeguards, not guarantees

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and operational measures designed to protect submitted information. No website, storage system, or transmission method can be guaranteed perfectly secure.

Retention

No fixed public retention schedule

The service does not currently publish a fixed automatic deletion schedule for stored session records. Local session data may clear when a review is reset or when stored browser data is cleared, and some anonymous local archives are capped by item count rather than time.

Policy updates

Public changes are posted here

As the product and data practices evolve, this page may change. When that happens, the Last updated line at the top of this page will change as well.

Privacy questions

If you have a privacy question about Operational Clarity or information you submitted through it, use the guidance on the Contact page and label the note clearly as a privacy question.

If the question relates to a previous submission, include enough detail to help identify it, such as the company name, the email used, and the approximate submission date.
